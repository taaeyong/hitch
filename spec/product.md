# Product Spec

## Working Goal
- Preserve Hitch core product identity and demo UX
- Extend Hitch beyond onboarding into ongoing relationship maintenance
- Add relationship-specific memory and wiki accumulation
- Support recurring check-ins rather than only cold-start quiz usage
- Keep the result compatible with future graph visualization

## Product Shift
v4 moves the center of gravity toward:
- post-onboarding recurring use
- relationship-specific check-ins
- memory accumulation
- wiki updates
- graph-ready structure
- long-term coaching relevance

## Product Principle
- Hitch behaves like a relationship understanding system, not just a quiz bot
- scheduled prompts help memory accumulate
- open conversational access lets the user ask for help anytime
- the combination of daily signals, periodic synthesis, and open-ended questions makes the wiki layer useful

## Tone and Manner
Hitch must stay:
- warm
- product-like
- emotionally legible
- not stiff
- not overly corporate
- not overly therapeutic
- not generic chatbot language

## Language Rule
- Korean by default
- English product/system terms may remain when more natural or direct

## Onboarding
### Goals
1. explain what Hitch is
2. explain why love can exist without landing well
3. explain why love-language differences matter
4. explain why repeated check-ins reveal hidden patterns
5. explain what the user can expect over time

### Length
- 4 to 5 short messages is acceptable
- depth is preferred over overly compressed onboarding

### Ordering
1. introduce Hitch as a relationship coach
2. relationship selection
3. partner invite option
4. basic setup collection
5. relationship space creation
6. love-language onboarding
7. final CTA
8. immediate start or later start choice

### Partner Invite
- should be optional and skippable
- may use QR, bot link, handle, or invite code
- must include a visible skip / 건너뛰기 option
- solo progression is allowed

### Final CTA
Preferred ending CTA:
- "지금 바로 시작해볼까요?"

### First Start Choice
After onboarding, allow:
- start immediately
- begin later at the configured daily time

## Love-Language Model
- shared core self-model
- relationship-specific deltas

This supports continuity of identity while allowing relationship-specific differences.

## Daily Flow
### Delivery Model
- long-term goal is bidirectional usage
- v4 first ships as a single-user simulation of that future model

### Question Categories
1. reflection
2. inference
3. action
4. understanding
5. playful scenario simulation

### Answer Depth
- daily answers should not remain too shallow by default
- the product should encourage enough detail for useful signal extraction

### Response Style
- Hitch replies with a short interpretive reflection
- not mere acknowledgement
- not a heavy action prescription every day

### Interaction Sequence
1. today's question
2. user answer
3. Hitch short interpretation
4. partner answer summary reveal
5. one-line alignment or gap note

## Weekly Flow
### Cadence
- weekly, not biweekly
- semi-structured

### Core Questions
1. what did you do this week to express love?
2. when did you feel the most loved this week?

- may add one follow-up question when needed

### Weekly Report
Sections:
1. one-line summary of the week
2. how love was expressed this week
3. when love was most strongly felt this week
4. alignment or mismatch points
5. interpretation of the week's relationship signal

### Weekly Tone
- warm but structured
- reflective without melodrama
- analytical without sterility

### Weekly Focus
- may include a one-line next-week focus only when signal is strong enough

### Weekly Confirmation
- ask for a light confirmation after the report
- must preserve Hitch tone and manner

## Open Conversation
- default mode is relationship-aware coaching
- use current relationship space and wiki only when it meaningfully helps
- avoid performative or forced references
- fall back to strong general relationship coaching when context adds little value
